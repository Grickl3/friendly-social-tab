Social Integrations

Should Be Friendly

A common anti-pattern in social media integrations, involves the violation of two design principles. These violations produce short term gains but play a non-negligible role in diminishing the longterm stability of the firm. One violation is of a principle of good user interface design and the other is a simple failure in respectful marketing.

The shape of these violations comes from using social media icons as sharing prompts. When a site visitor sees a social media icon in a sidebar, like the one on the left, it most commonly signals navigation to that firm's profile on the indicated platform. This is best practice.

Good user interfaces build relationship by making a comfortable space for exploration and ease of use. Joshua Porter may have said it best when he said, "Humans are most comfortable when they feel in control of themselves and their environment. Thoughtless software takes away that comfort by forcing people into unplanned interactions, confusing pathways, and surprising outcomes." His statement is an elegant explication of the Principle of Least Astonishment.

Marketing with respect is about helping people find solutions to the issues they're concerned about, rather than coercing or manipulating their decisions. Seth Godin puts it this way, "It's time to do something else with marketing. To make things better. To cause a change you'd like to see in the world. To grow a project, sure, but mostly to serve the people you care about."

Mad Men marketing may have been effective in the past; and in many cases it still is; though it is rapidly losing effect. But, it isn't appropriate for visionary leaders seeking the betterment of society or to increase the vitality of ecosystems.
